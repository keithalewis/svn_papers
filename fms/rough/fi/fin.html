Fixed Income Notation

The short rate process \((r_t)_{t\ge0}\) represents a series of instruments that cost 1 unit at time \(t\) and pay \(1 + r_t\,dt = \exp(r_t\,dt)\) at time \(t + dt\). The stochastic discount is defined to be \[ D_t = e^{-\int_0^t r_s\,ds}. \] If \((X_t)_{t\ge0}\) is the price of an instrument that pays no cash flows, then the fundamental theorem of asset pricing implies \((X_tD_t)_{t\ge0}\) is a martingale. Note that one unit invested at time 0 and rolled over at the short rate grows to \(1/D_t\) at time t. Clearly \((1/D_t) D_t = 1\) is a martingale.

Let \(D(t,u)\) be the price at time \(t\) of a zero coupon bond that pays 1 unit at time \(u\). Since \(t\mapsto D(t,u)D_t\) is a martingale we have \(D(t, u)D_t = E_t D(u,u)D_u = E_t D_u\) so \[ D(t, u) = \frac{E_t D_u}{D_t}. \] A forward starting zero coupon bond starting at \(u\) and terminating at \(v\) represents a zero cost contract at time \(t\) to pay \(D(t;u,v)\) at time \(u\) and receive a payment of 1 unit at time \(v\). This is equivalent to being short \(D(t;u,v)\) a zero coupon bond maturing at \(u\) and being long a zero coupon bond maturing at \(v\), hence \(0 = -D(t;u,v)D(t,u) + D(t,v)\) and so \(D(t;u,v) = D(t,v)/D(t,u)\).

A forward rate agreement is a zero cost contract at time \(t\) over the interval \([u,v]\) to pay 1 unit at time \(u\) and receive \(1 + F_\delta(t;u,v)\delta(u,v)\) at time \(v\), where \(\delta(u,v)\) is the day count fraction (approximately equal to \(v - u\) in years) for the interval. Scaling the payoffs by dividing by \(1 + F_\delta(t;u,v)\delta(u,v)\) is equivalent to a forward starting zero coupon bond so we have \(D(t;u,v) = 1/(1 + F\delta)\) hence \[ F_\delta(t;u,v) = \frac{1}{\delta(u,v)}(\frak{D(t,u)}{D(t,v)} - 1) \]
