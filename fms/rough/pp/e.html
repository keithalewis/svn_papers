Events

An event is a subset of a samples space.

The event of rolling an a face with an even number of pips on a six-sided die correponds to the subset \(\{2, 4, 6\}\).

The event that Brownian motion hits a barrier at level \(a\) by time 1 corresponds to the subset \(\{\omega\in C[0,\infty)\colon \max_{0\le t\le 1}\omega(t)\ge a\}\).

It is natural to talk about an event not happening, or two events both happening or either event happening. This goes back to George Bool and An Investigation of the Laws of Thought and leads to the notion of an algebra of sets.
