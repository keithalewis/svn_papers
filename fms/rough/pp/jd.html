Joint Distributions

If \(X\) and \(Y\) are random variables their joint distribution is \[ F_{X,Y}(x, y) = P(X\le x, Y\le y) = P(\{\omega\in\Omega\colon X(\omega)\le x\}\cap\{\omega\in\Omega\colon Y(\omega)\le y\}). \] If \(X_1,\dots, X_n\) are random variables, their joint distribution is \[ F(x_1,\dots,x_n) = P(X_1\le x_1,\dots, X_n\le x_n). \] Random variables having the same joint distributions are said to have the same law.
