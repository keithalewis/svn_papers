Brownian Motion

Brownian motion is a Lévy process having normally distributed increments. The Lévy parameters for a process with drift \(\mu\) and variance \(\sigma^2\) are \(\gamma = \mu\) and \(G(x) = \sigma^2 1_{[0,\infty)}\).

If \(B_t\) is standard Brownian motion (zero drift and variance 1) then every Brownian motion has the form \(\mu t + \sigma B_t\). In particular, this stochastic process can be characterized by just two numbers!

Recall that for any normal random variable, \(N\), we have \(E[\exp(N)] = \exp(E[N] + \Var(N)/2)\) so the characteristic function for Brownian motion at time 1 is \(\phi(u) = \log E[\exp(iu(\mu + \sigma B_1))] = i\mu u - \sigma^2u^2/2\).
