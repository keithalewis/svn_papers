Interest Rate Derivative Modeling

Interest rate derivative modeling is a triumph of mathematical finance. All major institutions use the theory for pricing, hedging, and managing the risk in by far the largest and most liquid instrument class as reported by the Bank for International Settlements.

Overview

A derivative security is a contract between a buyer and a seller to exchange one or more future cash flows. The buyer decides whether or not to initiate the transaction at the prices determined by the seller. In a two sided market, the buyer pays the ask/offer or receives the bid. These prices also depend on the size of the transaction and the credit worthiness of the buyer and seller, among other things.

The Fundamental Theorem of Asset Pricing determines the value of uncertain future cash flows (pricing) by describing how to move them back to today (hedging) with information about how accurately that can be done (risk management).

In this course you will learn how to use the FTAP to price, hedge, and manage the risk of fixed income derivative securities.
