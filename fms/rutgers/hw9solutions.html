Exercise 1. Find the credit spread of a one year zero coupon bond assuming a risk free rate of 1%, a default intensity of 5%, and 50% recovery.

Since \(D_{T,R}(s, t) = D(s,t)E[1(T > t) + R1(s\lt T\le t)]\) we have \(D_{T,R} = e^{-0.01}(e^{-0.05} + 0.5(1 - e^{-0.05})) = .9659 = e^{-0.01 - s}\) and \(s = 0.0247\).

Exercise 2. Consider an option on a risky 1 year zero coupon bond having $100 notional that pays $1 if the bond is trading between $10 and $90 one day prior to expiration. Assuming the risk free rate is 1%, the default intensity is 5%, and recovery is zero, what is the value of this option rounded to the nearest penny.

The value of the option is \(E[1(0.1 < D(t,1) < 0.9)D_t]\) where \(t\) is one year minus one day. Recall \(D_{T,R}(s,t) = D(s,t)((1 - R)P(T>t|T>s) + R)1(T > s)\) so with \(R = 0\) \(D(t,1) \approx P(T > 1 | T > t)1(T>t)\). Hence the bond will be trading at either 0 or 100 one day before expiration so the option is worthless.

The Plackett family of copulas are given by \[ C_\theta(u,v) = \frac{1 + (\theta - 1)(u + v) - \sqrt{[1 + (\theta - 1)(u + v)]^2 - 4uv\theta(\theta - 1)}} {2(\theta - 1)} \] for \(\theta\ge0\) and \(C_1(u,v) = uv\). Note that \(C_0(u, v) = \max\{u + v - 1, 0\}\) and \(C_\infty(u, v) = \min\{u, v\}\) give the bookends of the Frech\'et-Hoeffding inequality.

Given two random variables \(X\) and \(Y\) the odds ratio is \(\theta(x,y) = P(X>x,Y>y)P(X\le x,Y\le y)/P(X>x,Y\le y)P(X\le x,Y>y)\). Note that if \(X\) and \(Y\) are independent, then \(\theta = 1\), if \(Y = X\) then \(\theta = \infty\), and if \(Y = -X\) then \(\theta = 0\) for all \(x\), \(y\).

Exercise 3. If \(U\) and \(V\) are two random variables uniformly distributed on the unit interval having a Plackett joint distribution, show the odds ratio \(\theta(u,v)\) is constant.

Note that \(\theta(u,v) = (1 - C(u,1) - C(1,v) + C(u,v))C(u,v) /(C(1,v) - C(u,v))(C(u,1) - C(u,v)) = (1 - u - v + C(u,v))C(u,v)/(v - C(u,v))(u - C(u,v))\). Solving the quadratic for \(C(u,v)\) gives the Plackett formula.

Exercise 4. Consider a one year floating leg of a risky swap no recovery and payment frequency \(f\) times per year. Let \(V(f) = \sum_{j=1}^f (D(0,(j-1)/f) - D(0,j/f))P(T > j/f)\). Show \(V(2) \ge V(1)\).

\begin{aligned} V(2) - V(1) &= (1 - D(0,1/2))P(T>1/2) + (D(0,1/2) - D(0,1))P(T>1) - (1 - D(0,1))P(T>1)\\ &= (1 - D(0,1/2))P(T>1/2) + (D(0,1/2) - D(0,1))P(T>1) - (1 - D(0,1/2) + D(0,1/2) - D(0,1))P(T>1)\\ &= 1 - P(T>1/2) + D(0,1/2)(P(T>1/2) - P(T>1)) > 0\\ \end{aligned} since \(t\mapsto P(T>t)\) is decreasing.
