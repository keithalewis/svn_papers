Suppose \((F_t)_{t\ge0}\) is a forward rate process expiring at time \(u\) that is an Ito diffusion satisfying the SDE \(dF_t/F_t = \sigma(F_t, t)\,dB_t\), \(F_0 = f\). Let \(C(k,t;f) = E_t(F_u - k)^+\) be the forward price of a caplet. Recall \(\phi(k,t;f) = \partial^2 C/\partial k^2\) is the forward density of \(F_t\). The Fokker-Planck equation says the density satisfies \( \frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial f^2}(\sigma^2 f^2\phi) = \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t}\).

Exercise 1. Show \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial t} = \int_k^\infty (f - k) \frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial f^2}(\sigma^2f^2\phi)\,df\).

Since \(C(k,t;f) = E(F_u - k)^+ = \int_k^\infty (f - k)\phi(f)\,df\) we have \(\partial C/\partial t = \int_k^\infty (f - k)\partial\phi/\partial t\,df = \int_k^\infty (f - k)\frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial^2} {\partial f^2}(\sigma^2 f^2\phi)\,df\).

Exercise 2. Integrate the result from exercise 1 by parts twice to show \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial t} = \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2k^2 \frac{\partial^2 C}{\partial k^2}\).

Let \(u = f - k\) and \(dv = \frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial^2} {\partial f^2}(\sigma^2 f^2\phi)\,df\) so \(du = df\) and \(dv = \frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial} {\partial f}(\sigma^2 f^2\phi)\). Integrating by parts gives \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial t} = -\int_k^\infty \frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial} {\partial f}(\sigma^2 f^2\phi)\,df = -\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2 f^2\phi|_k^\infty = \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2 k^2\phi = \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2 k^2\frac{\partial^2 C}{\partial k^2}\).

This gives the Dupire formula for local volatility given call prices at all strikes and maturities: \(\sigma^2(k,t) = \frac{\partial C/\partial t}{(1/2)k^2\partial^2C/\partial k^2}\).
