Math 628 Sp12 Homework 3

Due Feb. 16, 2012 at 6:40PM

Your job is to provide a convincing argument that what you claim is correct.

Exercise 1. Calculate \(\Var \int_0^t B_s\,ds\), where \((B_s)\) is standard brownian motion.

Exercise 2. Prove \(M_t = \exp(-\frac{1}{2}\int_0^t \theta^2(s)\,ds + \int_0^t \theta(s)\,dB_s)\) is a martingale, where \(\theta\colon\R\to\R\) is a function. Show \(E_t[\exp(\int_t^u \theta(s)\,dB_s)] = \exp(\frac{1}{2}\int_t^u \theta^2(s)\,ds)\).

Exercise 3. Prove \(\int_t^u \sigma(s)B_s\,ds = \Sigma(u)B_u - \Sigma(t)B_t - \int_t^u \Sigma(s)\,dB_s\), where \((B_s)\) is standard Brownian motion and \(\Sigma'(s) = \sigma(s)\).

Exercise 4. Given the time varying Ho-Lee short rate process \(r_t = f(t) + \sigma(t)B_t\), find a closed form formula for \(D(t, u)\), the price at time \(t\) of a zero coupon bond maturing at time \(u\).
(Hint: Use exercises 2 and 3 and insert the term \(0 = -\Sigma(u)B_t + \Sigma(u)B_t\) to get the integrand \(\int_t^u (\Sigma(s) - \Sigma(u))\,dB_s\).)
