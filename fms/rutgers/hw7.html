Your job is to provide a convincing argument that what you claim is correct.

Suppose \((F_t)_{t\ge0}\) is a forward rate process that is an Ito diffusion satisfying the SDE \(dF_t/F_t = \sigma(F_t, t)\,dB_t\), \(F_0 = f\). Let \(C(k,t;f) = E(F_t - k)^+\) be the forward price of a caplet. Recall \(\phi(k,t;f) = \partial^2 C/\partial k^2\) is the forward density of \(F_t\). The Fokker-Planck equation says the density satisfies \( \frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial f^2}(\sigma^2 f^2\phi) = \frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t}\).

Exercise 1. Show \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial t} = \int_k^\infty (f - k) \frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial f^2}(\sigma^2f^2\phi)\,df\).

Exercise 2. Integrate the result from exercise 1 by parts twice to show \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial t} = \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2k^2 \frac{\partial^2 C}{\partial k^2}\).

This gives the Dupire formula for local volatility given call prices at all strikes and maturities: \(\sigma^2(k,t) = \frac{\partial C/\partial t}{(1/2)k^2\partial^2C/\partial k^2}\).

Exercise 3. Formal application of the Ito formula gives \(d(F_t - k)^+ = 1(F_t \ge k)dF_t + (1/2)\sigma^2 F_t^2 \delta_k(F_t)\,dt\), where \(\delta_k\) is the delta function having mass 1 at \(k\). Take the expectation conditioned on \(F_t = k\) to show \(\partial c/\partial t = E[\sigma^2\mid F_t = k](1/2)k^2\partial^2 c/\partial k^2\).

This gives the Derman and Kani result \(\sigma^2(k,t;f) = E[\sigma^2\mid F_t = k]\).

Exercise 4. Use \(f(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x - a) + \int_0^a f''(k)(k - x)^+\,dk + \int_a^\infty f''(k)(x - k)^+\,dk\) to show (1/2)\(E(F - a)^2 = \int_0^a p(k)\,dk + \int_a^\infty c(k)\,dk\), where \(p(k) = E(k - F)^+\) is the price of a put at strike \(k\) and \(c(k) = E(F - k)^+\) is the price of a call at strike \(k\).
