Exercise 1. For an in-arrears swap we need to calculate \(E F_j\delta_j D_j\). Show \(E F_j\delta_j D_j = E(F_j\delta_j + F_j^2\delta_j^2)D_{j+1}\). What can you conclude about the relationship between a regular swap and an in-arrears swap? [Hint: First show \(E_j(D_j - D_{j+1}) = D_jF_j\delta_jD_{j,j+1}\).]

We have \(E_j(D_j - D_{j+1}) = D_j - D_jD_{j,j+1} = D_j(1 - D_{j,j+1}) = D_jF_j\delta_jD_{j,j+1}\) hence \(E F_j\delta_j D_j = E F_j\delta_j (D_{j+1} + (D_j - D_{j+1})) = E F_j\delta_j (D_{j+1} + E_j(D_j - D_{j+1})) = E F_j\delta_j (D_{j+1} + F_j^2\delta_j^2D_jD_{j,j+1}) = E F_j\delta_j (D_{j+1} + F_j^2\delta_j^2D_{j+1}) = E (F_j\delta_j + F_j^2\delta_j^2)D_{j+1} \). Since \(F_j^2\delta_j^2\ge0\) we always have the value of an in-arrears swap is greater than a standard swap.
