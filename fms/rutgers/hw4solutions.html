The Ho-Lee model with short rate process \(r_t = \phi(t) + \sigma(t)B_t\) has the closed form solution \(D(t, u) = \exp(-\int_t^u \phi(s) - \frac{1}{2}(\Sigma(s) - \Sigma(u))^2\,ds - (\Sigma(u) - \Sigma(t))B_t)\) for the price at time \(t\) of a zero coupon bond maturing at time \(u\).

Exercise 1. If \(D(0,t) = \exp(-\int_0^t f(s)\,ds)\) show \(\phi(t) - f(t) = \sigma(t)(t\Sigma(t) - \int_0^t \Sigma(s)\,ds)\). What does this work out to when \(\sigma\) is constant?

We have \(f(t) = d(-\log D(0,t))/dt = \phi(t) + \int_0^t (\Sigma(s) - \Sigma(t))\sigma(t)\,ds = \phi(t) + \sigma(t)(\int_0^t \Sigma(s)\,ds - t\Sigma(t))\). If \(\sigma(t) = \sigma\) is constant, then \(\phi(t) - f(t) = \sigma(t\sigma t - \sigma t^2/2) = \sigma^2 t^2/2\).
